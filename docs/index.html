Philippine Departmental Budget Reviews

Budget evolution and absorptive capacity, FY 2018-2026

These reviews go beyond the headline figures. For each department we compile the proposed (NEP) and enacted (GAA) budgets down to the level of individual Programs, Activities, and Projects (P/A/Ps), then match them against the agency Financial Accountability Reports (the SAAODB) that record how each peso was actually released, obligated, and disbursed. Assembling these sources at the P/A/P level is painstaking, but it is what makes a sharper reading possible — not just how much a department received, but where Congress redirected it, which programs could absorb their funding, and where promised spending never materialized. Every report pairs the analysis with sortable, exportable reference tables so you can trace the figures yourself; coverage and scope vary by agency, as each report’s data note explains.

DAR
Department of Agrarian Reform
Office of the Secretary

The smallest department, and the one Congress trims rather than augments. A flat ₱8–10B budget for eight years jumps 62% in 2026 — built almost entirely on foreign-assisted loan projects that are the agency’s weakest absorbers.

  • Flat ₱8–10B for 8 years → ₱16.8B in 2026 (+62%)
  • Congress is a net subtractor: −₱2.4B (2022), −₱4.5B (2023)
  • Foreign-assisted projects 10% → 37% of budget, but disburse just 19–38%

DA
Department of Agriculture
OSEC + 9 attached agencies

The clearest case of appropriation outrunning execution. The OSEC budget nearly quadrupled, but disbursement fell to 69% — with two giant lines (FMR, which DA never executes, and the LFP/FAP black box) crowding out a rice-dominated operational core. Adds deep-dives by service type and by commodity.

  • OSEC GAA ₱43.9B (2018) → ₱165.5B (2026), 3.8×; D/A fell to 69%
  • FMR ₱33B (zero DA execution) + LFP/FAP ₱72B = 63% of 2026 OSEC
  • Operational core ~75% PSS, ~63% rice; equipment & livestock absorb worst (33–41%)

DENR
Department of Environment and Natural Resources
Office of the Secretary

The one department whose budget has been frozen. Held flat at ₱16–20B for nine years — a ~30% real-terms cut — while Congress trims the proposal and guts the reforestation line, and the department shifts from capital works toward payroll.

  • Flat ~₱19B for 9 years — ≈₱13.6B in 2018 pesos (~30% real cut)
  • Congress trims; Forest Development cut ₱6.6B→₱2.4B
  • Capital Outlays fell 37%→12%; administration is the largest line (21%)

DSWD
Department of Social Welfare and Development
Office of the Secretary

An almost pure cash-transfer budget — 4Ps, Protective Services, and the Social Pension — where Congress reshapes the portfolio from within: trimming 4Ps, scaling up Protective Services, and inventing AKAP by appropriation alone.

  • GAA nearly doubled: ₱141B (2018) → ₱264B (2026)
  • Three cash programs = 74–88% of the budget; 93–95% MOOE
  • Congress redirects: Protective Services +₱116B, 4Ps −₱83B

DPWH
Department of Public Works and Highways
Whole department + operating units

A different angle: with no P/A/P-level execution data, this review traces DPWH’s budget back to 1988 and maps how Congress redirects money — every year — from the Central Office toward named district engineering offices.

  • 2019 line-item veto: ₱95.4B struck, ₱74B from the Central Office
